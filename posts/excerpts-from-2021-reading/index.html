Chancellor Merkel declared that Germany had signed up for €500 million ($592.65 million) and appealed to others to do their part. It was a revealing discussion. A sum of €500 million was either a lot of money or absurdly parsimonious. If it had chosen to do so, Germany could have borrowed the funds necessary to meet not just the immediate needs of COVAX, but the entire projected cost of vaccinating the world—estimated, as of May 2020, at $25 billion—and it could have done so at negative interest rates. Instead, it chipped in €500 million
— Adam Tooze, Shutdown (p. 294, loc. 4501-4505, read on 31 December 2021)
